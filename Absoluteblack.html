Granite information

  • Family: Gabbroic Anorthosite
  • Formed: Low crystallization of molten magma beneath the Earth's surface over millions of years
  • Distinctive features: Deep, Uniform Black Color providing a sense of opulence and sophistication.
  • Color: Rich, deep black.
  • Texture and granularity: Fine to medium grain texture, contributing to a smooth and polished finish. This fine granularity allows for detailed and intricate designs.
  • Location: India, South Africa, and Sweden
  • Strengths & Weaknesses: Durability, Versatility,Timeless Aesthetic, Maintenance
  • Applications: Kitchen Countertops, Flooring, Wall Cladding, Monuments and Memorials, Decorative Elements

ABSOLUTE BLACK

Absolute Black Granite's versatility and striking appearance have made it a staple in the world of design, offering an enduring and refined presence in diverse architectural applications.
